[JA] 間質性肺炎 [ENG] INTERSTITIAL PNEUMONIA *Recovered/Resolved* Case Narrative: An 81-year-old Japanese female patient (T.O.) with a medical history of hyperlipidaemia (since 2003), hypertension (since 2006), and type 2 diabetes mellitus (since 2003, diet-controlled) was receiving MEVALOTIN (pravastatin sodium) 20 mg once daily by oral route since July 2008 for the management of hyperlipidaemia. Concomitant medications at the time of the event included AMLODIPINE 5 mg once daily (for hypertension, commenced January 2008) and LOSARTAN POTASSIUM 50 mg once daily (commenced March 2008). On 09 October 2008, approximately 15 months after initiation of MEVALOTIN therapy, the patient developed acute-onset dyspnoea, dry cough, and low-grade fever (37.8 degrees C). She was admitted to hospital and chest computed tomography (CT) revealed bilateral ground-glass opacities with sub-pleural consolidation consistent with drug-induced interstitial pneumonia. Pulmonary function tests showed a restrictive pattern (FVC 62% predicted). Bronchoalveolar lavage (BAL) demonstrated lymphocytic alveolitis with a CD4/CD8 ratio of 0.9. Serum LDH was elevated at 412 U/L. Drug lymphocyte stimulation tests (DLST) were performed for MEVALOTIN, AMLODIPINE, LOSARTAN, and LASIX; all results were negative. MEVALOTIN was immediately discontinued upon hospitalisation. The patient was treated with systemic corticosteroids (prednisolone 40 mg/day, tapered over 8 weeks). Chest CT at 4-week follow-up demonstrated marked improvement in bilateral ground-glass opacities. The patient showed progressive clinical improvement and was discharged on 01 December 2008 after 54 days of inpatient hospitalisation. Based on the temporal association between MEVALOTIN initiation and onset of interstitial pneumonia, the radiological pattern, BAL findings, and clinical improvement following drug withdrawal and corticosteroid therapy, drug-induced interstitial pneumonia secondary to MEVALOTIN (pravastatin sodium) is considered the most likely diagnosis. Causality is assessed as related by the reporter.
